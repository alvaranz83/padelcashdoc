    • Q1 - What does “minimun PADEL received” means on the trading box of PancakeSwap?


    A1 - With Multiple orders placed simultaniously there might be some price volatility at the time you place your transcation. The “minimun received” amount guarantees that in the case of high price movements during your order you should receive that amount of Padel as aminimun. Otherwise if the amount would be less your order would be reverted and not processed.

    Q2 - What does “Price Impact” means on the trading box of PancakeSwap?


    A2- Price impact is the difference between the current Market price of PADEL and the impact that your trade has on the price.

    Q3 – What is the Liquidity Provider Fee?


    A3 – Liquidity providers are external individuals or companies that provide Liquidity so that a digital currency can be traded. They do that by adding a pool with 2 digital currencies so that they can be traded, and that becomes what is called pair trading. In the case of PADEL, the Liquidity Pool is made of PADEL and BUSD so that they can be traded for one other, hence have liquidity.

    Decentralised exchanges reward Liquidity providers with a small % of each transaction for locking their funds and help provide liquidity of digital currencies.

    Q4- What Does “Default Transaction speed (GWEI)” means?:

    A4 - Each transaction on the blockchain needs to be validated by nodes, the gas fee is a small fraction of each transaction that goes as a reward for blokchain validators. Its default rate is about ~0.25% of the amount that you’re trading.

    If many transactions are being requested to the blockchain at particular times, you might need to pay a higher gas fee for your transaction to go through.

    Q5- What Does “Slippage Tolerance” means?:



    Slippage happens when traders buy or sell their assets more or less than the expected price. In its simplest meaning, slippage is the difference between a trade’s expected and actual price. It is quite a common occurrence when buying and selling cryptocurrencies. When you trade, you have in mind a specific price at which you will buy or sell. However, because the crypto market moves fast, the price can change when your order enters the market and when the trade is completed. You can thus end up buying or selling at a higher or lower price than you had intended. Slippage pertains to that difference between the price you had in mind and the price you had to settle for.

    In Pancakeswap, the default percentage usually falls between 0.5% to 1%. But you can go to settings and adjust the percentage of your allowed slippage when making transactions.

    Q6 – How can I add the Binance Smart Chain Network to my Metamask Wallet?

    A6 – Here is a Stpe-by-Step Guide on how to add Binance Smart Chain to your Metamask Account for both Metamasks’ Browser Extension and Mobile App.

    Step 1: Open MetaMask Browser Extension Open the MetaMask browser extension by pressing the MetaMask icon on the top right-hand side of your screen.



    Step 2: Add Network

    After logging into your MetaMask account, visit the network selection menu on the upper right side of your screen. The menu reads “Ethereum Mainnet” if it’s your first time switching networks. Click it to access a drop-down menu and hit the ‘Add Network” tab to proceed to the next step.



    Step 3: Enter BSC Details After clicking the “Add Network” button above, a new window will open.



    Enter the BNB address below into their relevant fields:

    Network Name – Smart Chain

    • New RPC URL - https://bsc-dataseed.binance.org/
    • Chain ID – 56
    • Currency Symbol – BNB
    • Block Explorer URL (Optional) - https://bscscan.com

    Tip: You are more likely to make errors typing the details into the fields. Therefore, do copy and paste them to eliminate typing errors.*



    You will realize that your mainnet network has turned into “Smart Chain”.

    If you wish to change back to the Ethereum network, simply press on the “Smart Chain” menu on the top.

    Adding BSC to MetaMask Mobile App

    Connecting to Binance Smart Chain using the MetaMask mobile app differs from the BSCscan and browser extension options. Also, BSCscan lacks the option of interacting with the MetaMask mobile app. Here is a step-by-step process of adding BSC to the MetaMask app.

    Step 1: Open Your MetaMask Mobile App

    Navigate to the MetaMask App on your smartphone, and open it. If you don’t have the app yet, visit the Play Store or iOS to download it.

    Step 2: Click “Settings”



    After opening the app, press the three dashes on the top left side to open a new window. Click the “Settings” menu in the lower half of your screen.

    Step 3: Add Network



    After a new window opens, navigate down and select “Networks”. Press “Add Network” then “Custom Networks” to add the Binance Smart Chain network. Like in the browser extension, a window with five empty fields will appear.

    Copy and paste the details below into their relevant fields:

    • Network Name – Smart Chain
    • New RPC URL - https://bsc-dataseed.binance.org/
    • Chain ID – 56
    • Currency Symbol – BNB
    • Block Explorer URL (Optional) - https://bscscan.com

    Step 4: Save Changes

    After pasting the details appropriately, press the “Save” tab at the bottom to add BSC to your MetaMask wallet. Your Mainnet network menu will now read “Smart Chain.” Again, you can switch back to Ethereum by clicking the network menu. That’s it!
